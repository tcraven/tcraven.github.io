VR House Interior

Back in 2018 I spent some time experimenting with the Oculus Go VR headset. I noticed that stereoscopic photographs (where each eye sees a slightly different image) often looked blurry and unconvincing. The best looking images that I could find were computer rendered, where the stereo alignment was exactly controlled.

I embarked upon a project to create the interior of my own new-build house in VR. As it turned out, the VR version ended up being completed before the real-life house. I believe that the results are some of the best VR images that I had seen at the time.

I built all of the 3D geometry myself, paying close attention to the accuracy of the dimensions, shapes and materials of the objects in the scenes. Each 4K resolution stereoscopic image took around eight hours to render on an Nvidia 1070 Ti GPU. I used ray tracing, physically-based materials and lighting. Everything was created using Blender.

The images below can be viewed on any Meta Quest VR headset, although the process is a bit involved. First, visit this page in the Quest browser, then long press on an image to download it to the headset.

For best results, I recommend using the Pigasus VR Media Player to view the images, as this gives a much cleaner image than the viewer in the Files app. In order to get the image displaying correctly, you need to choose the following settings:

  • View Options > 3D > Stereo: Over Under
  • View Options > Surround > 360
